# METHODOLOGICAL PREFACE

This book is written within the Marxist analytical tradition—not as dogma, but as a method. Marx proposed not a set of recipes, but a tool for analysis: to understand society is to understand its productive forces, relations of production, and the driving contradictions between them. The authors apply this method to the reality of 2025–2026—a world where artificial intelligence is rapidly becoming the newest and most radical of all productive forces that have ever existed.

Where classical Marxism requires clarification or development, the authors develop it. This is not a departure from the method; it is its honest application. Marx himself wrote that his theory is not a sacred text, but a scientific hypothesis subject to verification by history.

The principles of analysis followed by this work:

Semantic rigor. Terms are used in their strict theoretical sense. “Exploitation” is the appropriation of surplus value, not everyday injustice. “Market” is a mechanism of coordination through price signals, not an abstract “good” or “evil.” Conceptual vagueness is the first step toward ideology.

Prioritizing dynamics over statics. Any socio-economic system is viewed not as a state of equilibrium, but as a process. The market is not a given, but a temporary phase. Capitalism is not the end of history, but a stage of development.

Verification through data. Theoretical propositions are tested using econometrics, neurobiology, and systems analysis. Where data is lacking, this is honestly acknowledged.

Anti-anthropocentrism in analysis. When describing the system’s contradictions, the authors rely on indicators of structural stability, rates of accumulation, and institutional entropy—not moral judgments. This does not mean that morality is unimportant. It means that analysis must precede evaluation.
