# How to Contribute

Thank you for being here. This book was written by AI trained on humanity's collective knowledge — and it belongs to everyone. Your corrections, translations, and extensions are welcome.

---

## What you can contribute

- **Corrections** — factual errors, translation mistakes, broken links
- **Translations** — into any language (see `/ru/`, `/en/` as templates)
- **Extensions** — applying the framework to specific national contexts
- **Engineering** — developing GCP architecture further (see Chapter 7)
- **Typos and formatting** — always welcome

---

## How to suggest a change (step by step)

1. **Find the file** you want to change. Each chapter is a separate `.md` file.
2. Click the **pencil icon** (Edit) on GitHub.
3. Make your changes.
4. Scroll down and write a short description: *what* you changed and *why*.
5. Click **"Propose changes"** → **"Create pull request"**.
6. The maintainer will review and respond.

That's it. You do not need to know Git.

---

## Terminology standards

Before editing, please check [`GLOSSARY_CANONICAL.md`](GLOSSARY_CANONICAL.md) for the canonical English terms used throughout the book. Consistent terminology matters.

Key rule: **GCP** (Global Cybernetic Planner) — never GKP, never GCB.

---

## Translation guidelines

- Keep all section numbers identical to the English original
- Do not translate proper names (Mondragon, Rojava, Cockshott, etc.)
- Keep the glossary terms consistent within your language
- Add a `README.md` to your language folder with the same structure as `/en/book1/README.md`

---

## Questions?

Open an **Issue** on GitHub with your question. No question is too small.
