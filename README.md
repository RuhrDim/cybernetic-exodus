# The Cybernetic Exodus

It is time for humanity to grow up — or face extinction.
This book is a sketch of what humanity must become. A vector of effort
for those who have the will to develop the text and the technologies.

A Marxist analysis of a post-capitalist civilization built around the
Global Cybernetic Planner (GCP) — a decentralized, distributed system
for coordinating production and distribution in the interests of people,
not capital.

The book was co-authored with AI systems (Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT,
DeepSeek, Perplexity, Qwen) and is distributed free of charge.

---

## Books

- **Volume 1: Critique and Transition** — a Marxist analysis of the
  crisis of capitalism, the mechanics of transition, and the
  architecture of the GCP. (en/book1_full.md)
- **Volume 2: Architecture of the Future** — human life within the
  system: hubs, reputation economy, life cycle, neurosociety. (en/book2_full.md)

The books can be read independently — but together they form a unified whole.

## Open Tasks

### 1. Text
The book should become a tool for agitation and a practical guide.
Needed:
- editing and development of the argumentation
- examples from real cooperatives and movements
- a section on practical assistance in establishing a cooperative
  (organizational, legal, technical)

### 2. Conceptual Framework
The key maxim of the GCP requires a rigorous dialectical definition:
**"the realization of the full potential of every person."**
What does this mean operationally? How can it be measured? How can it
be protected from interpretations that serve the interests of the system?

### 3. GCP Core (software)
A basic software tool for building, managing, and networking
cooperatives at the initial level.

Requirements:
- low hardware requirements
- simple to install
- intuitive to use

#### 3.1 Infrastructure
The Core uses any available network — from standard internet to
LoRa/Meshtastic — and adapts its operation to the network's
capabilities.

#### 3.2 Legal Assistance
Built-in instructions and templates for establishing and maintaining
a cooperative across different jurisdictions.

#### 3.3 Communication Network
Instructions for deploying communications for a cooperative at any
available level of infrastructure.

### 4. Reputation and Distribution

Fractal reputation is a replacement for money as the measure of
access to resources.

Unlike money, it does not accumulate passively and cannot be
inherited. Unlike a unified social credit score — it does not
collapse all human behavior into a single number.

It is a multidimensional profile of confirmed contribution: separate
for each field of activity. High standing in biology confers no
advantage when voting on urban planning. A master in pedagogy
receives recognition precisely where it has been earned.

Reputation converts into priority access to resources beyond the
basic level — equipment, projects, computing capacity. Basic goods
(food, housing, healthcare) are not reputation-dependent: they are
guaranteed to all.

Why this matters: to eliminate the monopoly on status, make
recognition genuinely earned, and prevent the formation of a new
elite — technocratic or otherwise.

Development needed:
- a plan for the gradual introduction of fractal reputation
- forms of resource distribution at the initial stage and in development

### 5. Hub Designer
An algorithm that — given input data and local conditions — generates
a technically and organizationally ready-to-implement hub project.
Not a wish list, but a concrete plan.
